Statutory Compliance Requirements under the Companies Act 2016 

Dear Sir/Madam,

_Duty to lodge BO Information with Annual Return_

Pursuant to Section 56 of the Companies Act 2016 (“CA 2016”), the Company is required to lodge its BO information together with its Annual Return to the Suruhanjaya Syarikat Malaysia (SSM) within 30 days from the anniversary of its incorporation date.

Please note that non-compliance or the provision of false or misleading information is an offence and, upon conviction, may result in:
•	Imprisonment up to 10 years, or
•	A fine not exceeding RM3,000,000, or both

Kindly note that we do not accept any responsibility for fines or penalties arising from late or inaccurate submissions.

_Duty to Notify Changes in BO Information_

Pursuant to subsection 60C(1) of the CA 2016, every company is required to ensure that its register of beneficial owners is kept up to date. Accordingly, we kindly remind you that:
•	You are obliged to notify us in writing within 14 days of any change to the particulars of the Company’s beneficial owner(s), or if any person ceases to be or becomes a beneficial owner.

If we do not receive a response, we will assume that there are no changes to the previously submitted BO information, and that the existing details remain valid, accurate and confirmed to be true to the best of your knowledge and belief.

_Duty to Lodge Annual Return_

We hereby notify you that the Company is required to lodge its Annual Return with the SSM no later than 30 days from the anniversary of its incorporation.

Under Section 68(1) of CA 2016, every company is required to file its annual return annually, and the return must contain all particulars specified under Section 68(3) of the Act.

Failure to comply constitutes an offence, and upon conviction:
•	A fine not exceeding RM50,000; and
•	In the case of a continuing offence, a further fine not exceeding RM1,000 per day may apply.
This notice serves as a formal reminder of the obligation. We will not be responsible for any penalties incurred due to late submission.

_Duty to Disclose Directors’ Statutory information_

We also take this opportunity to remind all directors of their ongoing disclosure obligations under CA 2016:

a.	Changes in Interests - Section 59 & Section 219
Directors must notify the Company in writing within 14 days of any change in their interests (e.g. shares, debentures, options, contracts, etc.).

Failure to comply may result in:
•	Imprisonment for a term not exceeding 5 years, or
•	A fine not exceeding RM3,000,000, or both (Section 219(1)); and
•	For failure to notify, a fine not exceeding RM25,000, and a continuing fine of RM1,000 per day (Section 219(2)).
The Company must notify all other directors within 7 days of receiving such disclosure (Section 219(4)). Failure to do so is also an offence with a similar penalty.

b.	Disclosure of Conflict of Interest – Section 221
Directors must disclose any direct or indirect interest in contracts, proposed contracts, property, or offices that may pose a conflict of interest, as soon as practicable upon becoming aware.

Non-compliance may result in:
•	Imprisonment up to 5 years, or
•	A fine not exceeding RM3,000,000, or both.

c.	Disclosure of Other Directorships – Section 57
Directors must disclose their directorships in public companies and subsidiaries of public companies, excluding related companies.

Failure to comply may result in:
•	A fine not exceeding RM10,000, and
•	A further fine of RM500 per day for continuing offences.
